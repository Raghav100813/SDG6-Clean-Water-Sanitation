SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS

On 25th September 2015, 193 members' nation at UN General Assembly set up a collection of 17 goals known as "The Sustainable Development Goals", were adopted a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet and ensure that by 2030 all people would enjoy peace and prosperity.

SDG 6

CLEAN WATER & SANITATION

Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6) declares the importance of achieving “Clean Water & Sanitation for All”. It is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by the United Nations (UN) General Assembly to succeed the former Millennium Development Goal (MDGs).

According to United Nations, the overall goal is to: “Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.” The goal has 8 targets to be achieved by 2030 covering the main areas of water supply and sanitation and sustainable water resource management
